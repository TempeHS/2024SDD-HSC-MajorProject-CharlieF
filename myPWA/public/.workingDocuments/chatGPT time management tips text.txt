Set specific goals: Setting specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) goals provides clarity and direction. When you know exactly what you're working towards, it becomes easier to plan your time effectively. Break down your larger goals into smaller, actionable steps, and assign deadlines to each step. This not only makes your goals more manageable but also helps track progress along the way.

Use time blocking: Time blocking involves allocating specific blocks of time for different activities or tasks throughout your day. Instead of bouncing between tasks haphazardly, you dedicate focused periods to specific activities. This can help you maintain concentration, increase productivity, and prevent procrastination. It's essential to be realistic when scheduling blocks of time and to allow for flexibility in case unexpected tasks arise.

Minimize distractions: Distractions are productivity killers.
